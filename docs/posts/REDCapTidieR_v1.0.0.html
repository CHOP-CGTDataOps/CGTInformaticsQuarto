Development Journey

The humble beginnings of REDCapTidieR started with a single request to compile multiple REDCap projects for clinical trials into an analytic visualization tool. It became quickly apparent after working with the first couple of longitudinal databases that we were repeating the same operations to tackle REDCap‚Äôs standard, notorious block matrix and that no real solutions existed in the open source R community for handling complex REDCap databases that suited our needs.

To write the package, some core decisions had to be made up front. As a team rooted in tidy data principles, we wanted to ensure that outputs from our package corresponded with definitions for tidy rectangular data structures. Since the block matrix fundamentally obscures granularity in projects containing repeating events and repeating instruments, it was important to identify the composite primary keys that defined tables of varying project structures and isolate them from the block matrix while keeping them easily joinable.

We wanted a way to not only break out instruments into tidy tables, but also to house them under an easy-to-use structure. Thus the supertibble was born to leverage the accessibility of tibble‚Äôs elegant dataframes with the flexibility of nested list elements. This gave us even more room to grow by providing metadata to our users for quick exploratory data analysis of their projects.

Once the core concept was ironed out, REDCapTidieR was further adapted to give users more tools to work with their exported databases, including labelling their data with the labelled package, exporting their data to Excel with openxlsx2, and getting summary statistics with skimr.
